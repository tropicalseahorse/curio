The Mughal road that outlived three empires

Sher Shah Suri rebuilt the Grand Trunk Road in the 1540s, lining 2,500 kilometres with wells, shade trees and rest houses spaced a day's ride apart. The Mughals inherited it, the British paved it, and it still carries traffic from Kabul to Chittagong today — one piece of infrastructure that three successive empires each found easier to maintain than to replace.

What you get

Nothing without a source

Every card is written from full source text, then audited claim by claim before it reaches your feed. If a sentence isn't supported by the source, the card doesn't ship. No card exists without a link you can go check yourself.

Swipes, not sessions

About 150 words a card — one real idea, finished. Keep what's worth keeping, pass on the rest, and stop whenever you like. Your kept pile is the part you actually walk away with.

A feed that shows its work

Ten or fifteen swipes in, the feed noticeably re-tunes — and tells you which topics moved and why. Every topic keeps a floor weight, so narrowing your interests never narrows your world.
